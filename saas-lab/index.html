What it is

A studio for finding ideas worth building

SaaS Lab is my personal research and development studio. It exists to turn software ideas into real, testable products — fast enough that reality can answer before months of planning do.

Build quickly. Test with real users. Learn from the results. Repeat.

It is equal parts laboratory, incubator, portfolio, and learning platform. Every project exists to answer a question: Can this solve a real problem? Will people use it? Can it become a business?

If the answer is yes, it grows into a standalone product. If not, the experiment is documented, the lessons are kept, and the next idea begins. Failure is not wasted effort — it is research.

Why it exists

Ideas are cheap. Evidence is rare.

Most software ideas never become products because people polish for months before learning whether anyone wants the thing.

Core mission

Get better at choosing what deserves to exist

Build as many meaningful software experiments as possible while continuously improving the process of discovering valuable ideas.

The goal is not to launch hundreds of products. The goal is to become exceptionally good at identifying which ideas deserve to become products.

Philosophy

Every project begins with a hypothesis

The goal is never to prove the hypothesis correct. The goal is to discover the truth as quickly as possible — even when the truth is that the idea is terrible.

“People struggle to organize AI workflows.”

Test whether structure beats more prompts.

“What if health tracking became competitive?”

Test whether rivalry creates consistency.

“What if anyone could publish AI-powered workflows without APIs?”

Test whether existing subscriptions are enough.

“What if software ideas themselves became structured assets?”

Test whether organization improves judgment.

Development approach

Momentum over perfection

Ask the smaller question first

Instead of asking how to build the perfect version, SaaS Lab asks a narrower one:

What’s the smallest version that answers the biggest question?

Complexity is earned. It is never added by default.

Default materials

  • One-page applications
  • SQLite when possible
  • PHP and plain JavaScript
  • Minimal infrastructure
  • Simple deployments
  • Rapid iteration loops

Experiment lifecycle

Every project is an experiment

Each idea moves through the same disciplined path. Launch is one possible outcome — not the only valuable one.

01

Observation

Identify an interesting problem. Research existing solutions. Understand the market and the people inside it.

02

Hypothesis

Write a simple assumption. Example: users would rather use AI subscriptions they already pay for than expensive APIs.

03

Prototype

Build the smallest usable version. Not polished. Not complete. Just enough to test the idea.

04

Validation

Real people use it. Feedback is collected. Behavior matters more than opinions.

05

Iteration

Improve what users actually care about. Ignore everything else.

06

Decision

Choose one of three outcomes. Every outcome is valuable.

More than a portfolio

Show the evolution, not only the launch

Authentic record

SaaS Lab does not only display finished work. It shows how products evolve — where ideas began, how they changed, what failed, and what earned the right to continue.

  • Origins and early assumptions
  • Decision trails and tradeoffs
  • Failures with lessons attached
  • Successes with evidence

Build in public

Many developers only show success. SaaS Lab documents the process because transparency teaches. Visitors can watch software evolve over weeks and months.

That creates trust. It also demonstrates product thinking — not just programming ability.

The role of AI

AI increases velocity. Judgment stays human.

Artificial intelligence accelerates experimentation inside SaaS Lab — research, concepts, UX review, competitor analysis, feature brainstorming, documentation, interface prototyping, copy, and code review.

It does not replace development. The human remains responsible for taste, priorities, and final decisions.

  • Market research
  • Concept generation
  • UX critique
  • Competitor analysis
  • Documentation
  • Rapid prototyping

Long-term vision

A studio that turns curiosity into software

The long-term goal is a self-sustaining product studio that continuously discovers, builds, validates, and launches useful software — without betting everything on a single startup.

Most ideas will never become businesses. Some will become open source. Some will become internal tools. A few may become sustainable products. The value is the disciplined system that keeps converting curiosity into software, and software into knowledge.

SaaS Lab is not another product. It is the engine that produces them — the umbrella under which SousMeow, future AI tools, health competitions, creator utilities, and developer experiments begin before they graduate, pivot, or archive.
