The workshop

A public place to learn in the open

SaaS Lab is my public workshop.

Some ideas become products. Some become open source. Some fail. Every project teaches something.

Instead of hiding unfinished work, I use this space to document experiments, prototypes, and lessons learned while exploring new software ideas.

The goal isn’t to launch everything. The goal is to find the ideas worth building.

Operating method

Build Fast. Learn Faster.

A short loop for turning vague ideas into evidence.

01

Prototype

Build the smallest version possible.

02

Test

See whether real people actually want it.

03

Measure

Analyze what worked and what didn’t.

04

Iterate

Improve the promising ideas.

05

Archive

Not every experiment deserves to become a business. That’s part of the process.

Lab rules

Principles

A short set of constraints that keep experiments honest.

Build in public

Share the work while it is still unfinished.

Ship early

Put something real in front of people quickly.

Measure everything

Prefer evidence over instinct when deciding what continues.

Keep what works

Promote the ideas that earn their place.

Kill what doesn’t

Archive cleanly instead of dragging dead weight forward.

Learn continuously

Treat every outcome as material for the next experiment.
