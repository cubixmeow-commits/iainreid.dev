# Arcane Workshop Journal — Art Direction

The portfolio combines an adventurer's field journal with a practical inventor's workshop. The fantasy influence should come through materials, lighting, composition, and discovery rather than literal characters, weapons, spells, or franchise references.

## Core visual language

- Candlelit workshop atmosphere
- Dark oak, stone, brass, parchment, ink, wax, and blueprint paper
- Elegant book typography paired with modern readable interface text
- Engineering sigils, measured diagrams, engraved labels, and field notes
- Quiet motion: drifting dust, warm light, and restrained reveal effects

## Master image prompt

Create a cinematic fantasy-inspired inventor's workshop at night, viewed from slightly above a large dark wooden workbench. The room is built from old stone and dark oak, with restrained brass instruments, rolled plans, glass vessels, drafting tools, leather journals, shelves, and warm candlelight. The mood is intelligent, mysterious, handcrafted, and calm. Leave the central workbench area visually quiet and mostly empty so a responsive website interface can be placed over it. Use deep charcoal, walnut brown, aged brass, muted emerald, parchment ivory, and amber light. High-end environmental concept art, realistic materials, subtle atmospheric dust, dramatic but readable lighting. No people, no characters, no weapons, no dragons, no readable writing, no logos, no franchise symbols, no UI, no text, no excessive magical effects. Wide 16:9 composition.

## Mobile crop prompt

Use the same workshop and material language, but compose vertically around a single journal and a pool of warm light. Keep the upper and central area uncluttered for mobile text. No people, symbols, text, weapons, creatures, or recognizable franchise imagery. Vertical 9:16 composition.
